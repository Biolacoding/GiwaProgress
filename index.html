About Progress Jesutomiwa Giwa

Born in 2000 in Lagos, Progress Jesutomiwa Giwa is a Nigeria born multifaceted artist whose work bridges creativity and environmental advocacy, using art as a tool to promote sustainability and inspire positive change in communities.

He began as a student of art, drawn to the power of visual expression. But in 2021 his trajectory took a transformative turn when he joined the Western coast Maritime Academy (WECMA) as a cadet. He did a term paper research on the influx of plastic in the water bodies, this opened his eyes to the urgent issues facing our oceans, particularly the growing crisis of marine pollution. That awakening became the spark that ignited his mission.

Since 2023, he has dedicated using his art as a tool for environmental awareness and action, he believes art is a universal language with no barrier.

Progress's artistic journey has been marked by remarkable achievements, including exhibiting at Kongi @90, the celebration of Professor Wole Soyinka's 90th birthday, showcasing his work at the Global Entrepreneurship Festival 2024, He held his first solo exhibition, Waste to Luxury (2025), a powerful visual narrative that redefines waste through the lens of luxury and environmental responsibility.

He emerged as the winner of the Arts for Change Foundation Competition (2024 edition) and the Grand Winner of the Art Star Competition for environmental awareness organized by TwelveBrassStreet Atlanta, USA (2025). He also won the Lagos Has Talent – Climate Edition(2025), further affirming his voice as an artist with purpose.

In recognition of his contributions to environmental sustainability, Progress was appointed as the Youth Ambassador of the Lagos State Ministry of Environment.

Beyond creating in the studio, he is the founder of Susty creative hub where he runs initiative like The Recycle Art Tour an artistic initiative, using art as a tool to raise awareness about environmental issues—extending impact far beyond the studio walls in community, schools and organizations.
